# Mike King — YouTube Transcript

## Video: AI Search, Hyperpersonalization & The Future of SEO
Source: https://youtu.be/p0XPAM-kQUk?si=DleA5fG3nokiRowt

## Key Insights

**On Hyperpersonalization:**
Search is heading fast toward hyperpersonalization. The idea of a single 
SERP that everyone sees is going to evaporate. Content strategy needs to 
become persona-driven — one piece of content for everyone won't cut it.

**On SEO vs Search:**
"I don't care about SEO. I care about search." Most SEO work is basically 
an ETL process — extract, transform, load — which can and will be 
automated. SEOs who don't adapt will lose their place in the industry.

**On Automation:**
Much of what SEOs do manually — data scraping, transforming into 
spreadsheets, building reports — will be automated. Even content 
optimizations done qualitatively are actually quantitative tasks that 
machines will do better.

**On Staying Ahead:**
Mike's approach: build software, study how Google and AI platforms 
actually extract information from the web, and optimize in the same way 
the platforms themselves work. He references Google's LangExtract library 
as an example.

**On the Future:**
Enough with theory — the industry now needs data, case studies, and 
proof of what actually moves the needle. The next era of SEO belongs to 
those who experiment and build, not just those who talk.
