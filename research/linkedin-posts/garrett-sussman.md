## Post 1
Nostalgia is such a trap for SEO.

The old internet was weird, funny, personal, broken, and impossible to 
find half the time. But AI Search is creating something different.

Key insight: If I searched "Boston toddler activities" on Google, I'd 
get listicles. If I asked ChatGPT the same question with full context, 
I'd get something closer to an actual answer.

That's the part SEOs and marketers are struggling with. AI Search is 
creating real problems for publishers and content teams. But for 
consumers, getting a faster, more contextual answer is often better.

What this means for content strategy:
- Commodity content has less value
- Content needs to be more specific AND harder to create AND higher quality
- The work has to be more connected to real customer situations
